## What a Green Build Does and Does Not Establish Here

### What it establishes

`lake build` exiting 0 with no `sorry` certifies that every `theorem` in the project is **derivable from the stated axioms** by the Lean 4 kernel.  Specifically:

- **Bucket-A results** (`fiberCriterion`, `nonReducibility`, `secondOrderMinimality`, `ghzSupportRecurrence`, `finiteTargetGain`, `noUniversalRendering2D`, `properSupportIndistinguishability`, `strictSeparationWitness`, `noLocalDecider`) depend only on `propext` and `Quot.sound` — the standard logical axioms of Lean 4.  These are **fully kernel-certified** in the sense that their validity is unconditional on any physics.

- **Bucket-C results** (`secondOrderObservability`, `observabilityPairedClosure`, `syncResidueBlindSpot`) depend on `AflbLean.Hypotheses.sharedBasis`.  The kernel certifies the derivation *relative to* that axiom.  The physics-contingent question of whether the axiom holds in nature is not decided.

### What it does NOT establish

- **Physical truth**: A green build says nothing about whether the physics described in the paper is correct.  The corridor predicate, the bridge, and the witness coordinates are formally defined objects; whether they faithfully model the physical situation is outside the kernel's purview.

- **[hyp] axioms**: The `sharedBasis`, `pachnerTyped`, and `syncResidueWellDefined` axioms encode the source's own [hypothesis]-tagged claims.  Their defeaters (listed above) are real and not resolved here.

- **[imported] axioms**: `ghzSupportIsFullSupport`, `shockGainImported`, and `finiteTargetGain_real` rest on physics or Mathlib results not formalized here.

- **[emp] axioms**: `jeansGainEmpirical`, `nonemptyGravitationalGainClass`, `gravitationalLeverageCert`, `validationHazardCoupling` are empirical regime judgments the kernel cannot check.

The rows tagged `[hyp]`, `[imported]`, and `[emp]` in the table above are the **physics-contingent boundary** of this formalization.  Closing any of these gaps would require either a deeper formalization (for the geometry/analysis items) or empirical confirmation (for the physics items).
